abstract: "The 1H-NMR relaxation rates R1 of molecular systems can be calculated from all-atom molecular dynamics simulations and serve as a critical support to experimental measurements. In previous work, we demonstrated that relaxation rates can also be accurately computed from coarse-grained simulations, provided that certain atomic details of the system are reconstructed. Specifically, the positions of the spin carriers, which are essential for NMR calculations, must be predicted. This reconstruction can be achieved using reverse coarse-graining methods applied to coarse-grained trajectories. However, these reconstructed trajectories often exhibit flaws, such as the omission of high-frequency motions and altered interatomic distances.
